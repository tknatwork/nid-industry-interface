The core problem

The visible portal is the small part.

Industry Interface runs on ASP.NET Web Forms, with cycle dates hand-edited into the HTML and onboarding that still begins as an email. The workflow underneath, from first contact to a signed offer, carries caveats that never surface until a recruiter is already mid-cycle.

  • 01

    An undocumented workflow

    Onboarding, selection, and offers run on conventions the placement cell holds in its head, not in the system. New recruiters keep hitting rules they could not see.

  • 02

    A missing compliance spine

    Stipend floors, GST-correct receipts, data residency, an audit trail: government and institutional norms live in email threads and scanned PDFs, not in the product.

  • 03

    Portfolios that will not load

    The portfolio surface ships roughly 8 MB per discipline view, fetched one student at a time, so recruiters often cannot find a portfolio that is sitting right there.

  • 04

    Fragmented contact

    Two mailboxes, scattered phone numbers, no single status surface. "Did anyone read my email" becomes the recruiter's default state for weeks.

The thesis. Fix the workflow, not just the screens. Surface the hidden caveats, encode the compliance, and make the portfolio findable, without forcing the institute into a risky big-bang cutover.

How I worked with AI 02 / 04
02

How I worked with AI

AI did the reading. I held the judgment.

AI synthesised the public research, drafted the architecture, and wrote first-pass code across four build rounds. I decided what to build, what to refuse, and where a cheaper tool beats a clever one. The interesting calls were mine; the volume was the model's.

Decisions I made

  • AI translates, never judges

    The system maps recruiter vocabulary to NID disciplines and summarises long JDs. It never ranks, scores, or filters students. The line is drawn in the product, not just the prompt.

  • ML over LLM where the task is structured

    JD extraction, skill matching, scope-creep checks, stipend math: deterministic ML and pure formulas. The system stays light, fast, cheap, and self-hostable.

How the build stayed coherent

  • A context contract per module

    Every module carries a five-file markdown contract plus a git-backed memory layer, so any model could resume the build without re-reading the whole history.

  • A human gate on every merge

    Adversarial verification, strict TypeScript, and module-boundary checks. The model proposes; the gates and I dispose.

Why it reads as built, not generated. The choices that shape this portal are refusals: no fit-score, no bulk shortlist, no LLM where a formula will do. A first-draft copy-paste would have added features; the work was in taking them away.

The rationale

Why these choices, and what they protect.

AI translates, never judges
Design is subjective. A fit-score would collapse the surface that lets a surprising portfolio win. AI disambiguates and summarises; the recruiter decides.
ML over LLM where it counts
Structured work does not need a 70B model. Extraction, matching, and stipend math run as ML and formulas, so the system stays light, fast, cheap, and self-hostable on STQC-cleared infrastructure.
Individual-only shortlist
No select-all, a required note per student. The interface makes real evaluation the path of least resistance.
Mobile-first for the interview room
The console is a web app on a phone. Day-of work happens with a device in hand, so scoring, advancing, and pinging the coordinator are built for that context first.
Brand parity with nid.edu
The recruiter should perceive one institution. Open Sans, the nid.edu blue, and the discipline palette are deliberate. Identity preservation is credibility.
